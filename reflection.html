Design Process & Methodology

üéØ

Research Approach Strengths

Our systematic literature review of behavior change psychology provided a solid theoretical foundation that distinguished our solution from purely technical approaches. This research led to our pivotal discovery that traditional leaderboard systems fail to create emotional connection with environmental impact.

Key Framework: Self-Determination Theory gave us clear design principles for autonomy, competence, and relatedness, ultimately guiding our shift from competitive rankings to collaborative tree planting games.

The comparative analysis of existing fitness and environmental apps revealed that most relied on boring leaderboards and competitive rankings. This insight led us to explore nature-based gamification through forest visualization, creating a unique approach that makes CO‚ÇÇ savings emotionally meaningful through growing trees.

Areas for Improvement

In retrospect, we could have conducted more extensive user interviews early in the process to discover the limitations of traditional leaderboard systems sooner.

  • Earlier direct user feedback on gamification preferences
  • More user-centered design approach from the start
  • Balance tech vs UX considerations with nature-based psychology
