Problem Statement

How do we make CO‚ÇÇ tracking engaging and move beyond traditional ranking systems to create meaningful environmental visualizations that motivate sustainable transportation choices? Traditional CO‚ÇÇ tracking apps rely on boring leaderboards and competitive rankings that disconnect users from the true environmental impact of their actions.

üî¨

Research Focus

Investigating user engagement psychology, forest visualization strategies, and game mechanics to create compelling CO‚ÇÇ tracking experiences that go beyond simple leaderboards and rankings.

Our approach is grounded in Self-Determination Theory and explores how social aspects can successfully translate into software systems and UX design.

üöÄ

Solution Approach

Developing a forest planting game that transforms communiMap data into engaging tree visualizations, moving beyond boring ranking systems to meaningful environmental storytelling through virtual forests. We created a comprehensive Figma prototype demonstrating how these features could be seamlessly integrated into the existing communiMap interface.

Project Context

This project is part of the broader GALLANT initiative, addressing one of the key challenges in promoting sustainable urban transportation. The need for visible, measurable impact from individual choices has never been more critical as cities work toward carbon neutrality goals.
