Project Overview

What we built: A forest planting game that turns community walking and cycling data into fun tree visualizations. No more boring leaderboards!

Why it matters: Making CO‚ÇÇ savings feel real and meaningful through growing virtual forests instead of staring at cold numbers.

The Challenge We Tackled

Figuring out the problem and coming up with solutions that actually work

The Problem

Main issue: How do we make CO‚ÇÇ tracking actually fun and move away from those boring ranking systems? We want to create something that helps people feel genuinely motivated about making sustainable transportation choices.

What we found: We looked at 12 existing CO‚ÇÇ tracking apps and found that 85% just use boring leaderboards where you compete with random people. When we talked to users, they told us these competitive rankings make them feel guilty or judged rather than excited about helping the environment.

The gap: Research shows that social features can motivate people to change their behavior, but nobody's really figured out how to make this work well in apps yet, especially for environmental stuff.

üî¨

What We Researched

We dove into what makes people tick when it comes to games, connecting with nature, and caring about the environment. We wanted to create CO‚ÇÇ tracking that goes way beyond boring leaderboards and actually gets people excited. Our research is based on psychology theories about what motivates people and we looked into how social features can actually work in apps.

üöÄ

Our Solution (That Actually Works!)

The cool part: We built a forest planting game that takes communiMap data and turns it into fun tree visualizations. Instead of competing with strangers, you grow beautiful virtual forests that represent your real environmental impact.

How we know it works: We tested it with 45 people and found that forest games were 65% more engaging than competitive leaderboards. Users said they felt "connected to nature" rather than "judged by other people."

What we built: We created a complete Figma prototype that shows how this would work with the existing communiMap app, but makes the whole experience way more motivating and fun.
